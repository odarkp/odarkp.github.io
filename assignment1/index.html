Luni Website

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to when interacting with the website was how well it immerses the user into their "brand world". First of all, Luni uses a very distinct and unique visual style, based on interactive 3D elements that respond dynamically to user input, such as hovering, through movement and scaling. The design style of the website itself is also visually appealing, making the experience fun and intriguing.

Additionally, the camera's perspective subtly shifts in response to the cursor's position, which intensifies the feeling of being immersed, almost as if you are inside a video game. Taking all of these elements into consideration, I was immediately met with a lot of excitement and motivation to continue exploring the website and its contents upon my first interaction.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Hover
  • Scroll
  • Click
  • Drag

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time interacting with the homepage interface. This is because of the eye-catching and visually appealing 3D models that appear when the user enters the webpage. Not only are these models visually appealing, most of them are also interactive. For example, under the homepage category "Omada", users can play their own football game by clicking the interactive football on the screen. If they manage to score a goal, an animation of bursting coins appears on the screen. These kinds of interactions make the website playful and increases the overall time spent by users on their website.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action in my two-minute interaction with the experience was scrolling and clicking with my mouse. As the webpage only focuses on a point-and-click based interaction that guides the user through the webpage, there is no search tool that, in my opinion, would make it easier for the user to navigate the website and locate specific information effectively.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience is to gain user interest. The company aims to create an interesting and exciting experience, so the user becomes more intrigued and builds an interest in finding out who Luni is and what they stand for.

Capturing the attention of potential clients through a simple and conventional website these days has become almost impossible in a highly saturated digital environment. Therefore, Luni has seemingly taken on an unconventinal approach, incorporating playable elements and interactions that distinguish the experience from more “standard” web designs. These “out-of-the-ordinary" features enhance engagement and make the website more intriguing.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

As Luni is a studio that focuses on B2C app development, marketing and branding for companies, I believe their website is great at attracting attention from potential customers. Their website's interactive and playable elements showcase that they are both passionate about their craft, and fully capable of creating immersive experiences for users through mobile applications. Even on their own website, they stick to their company's distinct design profile, which shows that they are very aware of the power of personal branding, an awareness that is very attractive for future clients.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

My impression is that the Luni website appears to be designed for long-term, ongoing engagement, rather than a one-time visit. The website itself operates as an entry point for users and potential clients to learn and experience how Luni works and what they are capable of creating. The website's interface also focuses on accessible, simple, user-friendly design in order to support continuous interaction. Additionally, it appears that Luni often updates their website with open career positions and news on app developments to entice users to re-visit the website over a long period of time.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The website does not explicitly communicate that it should be interacted with over time. The website provides no mechanisms or features that encourage users to return or engage with it repeatedly. Instead, they focus on creating a unique interface that piques the users' interest, so they might be willing to return to the website.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The website is clearly influenced by other media forms such as mobile applications. The digital interface contains multiple similarities to the structures and interfaces of apps. This includes playable elements (as mentioned in question 3), a simple and intuitive interface, and a user flow that guides you through the levels of information included on the website. As the website reminds me of video games, it evokes feelings of playfulness and happiness, feelings I believe Luni intends to give the user. This makes sense when you take into account that Luni is a company that makes profit from branding, marketing, and developing apps for companies.

10.What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

This communicates to me that Luni's use of mobile applications and games as media references encourages the users to spend more time on the website and get excited as they play through its interactive elements, at the same time as they are getting informed about the company. This creates exploratory behavior towards the website's contents, and suggests that the users are invited to participate, rather than simply reading information. The simple visual design creates a low-pressure form of interaction, where users can engage casually with the website, without the need of prior knowledge of the company. This makes the experience feel accessible and easy to return to regularly.

11.What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

This communicates to me that Luni's use of mobile applications and games as media references encourages the user to respond positively to the website. Based on their colorful visual style and interactive mechanics, they aim to evoke emotions such as happiness, excitement, nostalgia, and enjoyment. In my opinion, I believe they are successful in creating this emotional response, as these were the emotions I experienced as I interacted with their website.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

When you choose to read about the sub-categories included in the homepage, an overlay of text fills the screen, and the user has to scroll through the overlay in order to read everything. However, while scrolling, the overlay collapse button disappears, and thus, the user is stuck on the text overlay and loses the option to exit the reading experience. The only way to close this overlay is to scroll all the way back down or to the top. This frustration could easily be fixed by attaching a sticky top-bar to the overlay, so the user has the opportunity to exit the text overlay if they would like.

Additionally, there is no search tool that, in my opinion, would make it easier for the user to navigate the website and locate specific information quickly. Instead, the user is being forced to go along with the websites intended journey through multiple interfaces, in order to find the right information. This can be a somewhat frustrating experience if one wishes to find information effectively.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

In my opinion, the most satisfying part of the interaction is the overall flow of the website, both the user flow and the visual flow. When it comes to the user flow, it is clear to see that Luni has developed their website with the intention to guide the user through an informative and interactive journey, that creates a client interest with the user. The interface is very minimalistic and simple, which makes the UX (User Experience) intuitive and efficient. When it comes to the visual flow, the website is characterized by soft colors, beautiful 3D models, satisfying transitions and animations, and a uniform design profile, which makes the UI (User Interface) very appealing to the user.
